About

Hi, I'm Trevor! I'm studying software engineering at McGill University in Montreal. I've worked with a variety of languages and platforms over the past few years, but my passion is iOS app development. My focus is always on the user experience and not just about writing good code.

Currently, I'm working on a skateboard tracking app called Wheels. It takes advantage of a lot of very good Apple frameworks like location, sharing, and activities. It's been a fun project and one that I use quite often when I ride.

I'm also the lead software engineer for the flight computer on the McGill Rocket Team. It's an entirely student run club, and we're currently working hard to develop our next generation rocket. In the role, I help manage members tasks and also work on some embedded programming myself such as I2C communications and GPS buffer parsing. Check out my work section to find out more!

If you have any questions about my work, or want to see if we can work together, you can send me an email of find me on LinkedIn. I'm on Mastodon too, if that's your thing. All my open source code is on GitHub if you want to take a look. I'm always open to new projects and ideas!

Work

Here's a list of the work I've done over the years. This includes some of my passion projects and a few things I've developed for others

McGill Rocket Team

I'm the lead software engineer on the flight computer sub-project. I manage the other members of the FC project and give them advice when writing code. Written in a mix of C and C++, the FC is by far the biggest project I've worked on. I've worked a lot on sensor communication using I2C and low level buffer handling.

Links: website

Wheels

Wheels is a skateboard ride tracker that tracks your history of riding. When you're riding, you can see all your stats (speed, distance, duration). You can save your skateboard collection, including cruisers, longboards, and electric boards. You can join a group ride with others and view your riding status on the home screen with widgets and live activities.

Links: app store, website

AlwaysArt

AlwaysArt is a startup looking to connect artists, galleries, and collectors. I've been working on an iOS app to help manage inventory that includes server connections, QR scanning, and authentication. The app has plans to be released to the public but it's currently only available to AlwaysArt employees.
